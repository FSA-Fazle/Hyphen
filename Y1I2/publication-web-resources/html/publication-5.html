Bangladeshi Community News

Highlights 2025

2025 was eventful for the Bangladeshi community of Brantford. The com-munity organized some social/cultur-al events. Here are the highlights of those events.

Ekushey February

Feb 23, 2025

The Bangladeshi Community of Brantford celebrated International Mother Language day on Sunday, Feb the 23rd, 2025 at the Mount Pleasant Community Centre.

People showed up in the afternoon in their traditional dresses, mostly in black and white, keeping with the gravity and somber feeling of the event.

The stage was decorated by sticking large letters from different languages of the word on a black background. Tabassuma played the key role in dec-orating this backdrop. A replica of the Shahid Minar was built (by Sinem) and placed beside the stage.

There were cultural performances, by kids and adults; a play was staged by the kids: which was scripted and directed by Abu Haque; in broad strokes the play tried to visualize the unfolding of events that cul-minated to the sacrifice of students for upholding the honour of Mother Tongue. To pro-duce this play kids and their families had done consid-erable hard work, they attended rehearsals in pre-ceding four con-secutive weeks and showed real integrity and compassion. Abu Haque and Roksanara Begum played a pivotal role in keeping the energy up through the rehearsals.

A slide show was presented by Abu Haque summarizing the history and the significance of the Day.

On behalf of the Bangladeshi Com-munity Abu Noman Tarek invited Ward 1 Councillor Rose Sicoli (now deputy-mayor); she kindly accepted the invitation and showed up at the event. She was presented a photo

frame with the picture of Shahid Minar which was drawn by Nuba. In her short speech Mrs. Sicoli made the audience happy by promising that she would try to support the process of passing a By-law to Recognize Inter-national Mother Language Day by the City (the promised By-law was passed later in the Month). Bangla-deshi Community also requested her to initiate a plan in raising a Language Monument somewhere in Brantford, she promised that she would work to-wards that goal with the Community.

There were individual performances and group performances. Kids sang, danced, recited poems. Shomi and Sultana had a duet recitation of a Ban-gla Poem. Elma, Nuba had individual dance performances. Farin, Raiha each sang a song. Faiha, Rayed recited poems. Arham played a song on the violin. Tabassuma recited a poem. Raiena and Abu Haque had a duet song. Rashed, Shomit entertained the audience with their musical perfor-mances.

Abu and Sultana anchored the pro-gram, and with their charming per-sonality, jokes and anecdotes kept the audience engaged throughout the program. Shomit took care of sound and other technical support.

Food was served at the end of the pro-gram. All the expenses were through community contribution. The finance side of things was managed by Sajjad. Food catering, disposable cutleries, drinks and related items were sourced and arranged mainly by Taslim. n
