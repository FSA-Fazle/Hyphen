The planning for the event began months in advance, which includ-ed selecting the menu for the meals featuring traditional Bengali dishes like Panta-ilish and pitha, rehearsing for the songs, making arrangements for booking the hall, creating banners and festoons and all sorts of other ac-tivities. On the morning of the event, the organizers and volunteers arrived early to prepare the venue, while others joined later. The program began with a chorus song welcoming the day of the Bangla calendar, “Esho he boiskhakh…” and continued throughout the day with numerous performances from both adults and the kids. There were poetry recitals, dance, and songs.

The entire day was spent with food, drink, music, fun and laughter. This year there was a game introduced where participants divided in groups were asked to create a collage using the paper, glue, and other materials from their memory of the Pohela Boishakh and the winner was lat-er picked through group members' votes. Everyone joined in the fun, however, Raiha, Nuba, Farin, Faiha, Arham, Tabassuma, Raiena, Sultana, Abu Haque, Shomit, Kabya per-formed. Abu and Sultana conducted the proceedings, Tabassuma recited

Abu Haque gave a brief presentation on the history of Pohela Boishakh. He explained how these cultural events and festivals help the diaspora com-munity to negotiate their identity and potentially reconcile and manoeuvre through the hybrid in-between space of memory and living space. n

Bangladeshi Community Picnic

July 12, 2025

This year's picnic was special as it was a 2-days event starting with camping

and camp fire on Friday and continu-ing with the main Picnic Event on Saturday.

The preparation for the picnic this year started early with the slaughter of a goat a week before the picnic. The goat did not die in vain, instead it was

then used for printing on thermoses and some of the certificates given to students. The event was held at the Mount Pleasant Community Center on 21st of June, 2025. Food was pur-chased using the donations collected from the community and sales of thermoses.

The program started with an intro-duction from Tabassuma Bari. Sul-tana Jahan Dalia, Arham Hasan and Raiena Haque then took over to host the rest of the program — they all did a fantastic job presenting. It was followed by the National Anthem of Bangladesh sung by all the students. After the National Anthem, Nuba per-formed a dance to the song “Hridoy amar Bangladesh”. Farin followed Nuba’s performance with a song “Alo amar, alo Ogo” which was followed by “Aji Joro Jhoro Mukoro” sung by Raiha.

With the songs and dance complet-ed, Faiha took the stage to recite the poem “Daat Khani Chaal” followed by a piano performance of the song “Amar Shonar Bangla” by Kotha. All the students then took the stage to preform “Grisho Borsha” to bring an end to all the performances for the day.
